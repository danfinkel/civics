Boston-area public schools — generic registration packet (SYNTHETIC)

Before your child can be enrolled, the school registration office must verify age, Boston residency, and health compliance. The list below is a simplified training checklist; wording and requirements may differ from any real district form.

1. Proof of age

  • One official document showing the student’s date of birth (for example: birth certificate, passport, or immigration record).
  • Copies must be clear and legible; bring originals if staff requests verification.

2. Proof of Boston residency — two documents, two categories

  • Provide two different residency documents from different categories (for example: a lease plus a utility bill).
  • Two items from the same category (for example, two leases for the same address) typically do not satisfy the “two proofs” rule.
  • Acceptable categories often include: lease or mortgage; utility bill; city-issued residency card; notarized affidavit from a host family (when applicable).

3. Immunization record

  • Complete vaccination history from a licensed provider, including required doses for school entry.
  • Commonly requested vaccines include MMR, DTaP, varicella, and hepatitis B, with dates of administration.

4. Grade / program placement (if applicable)

  • Prior school records or report cards may be requested to confirm grade level.
  • Students entering certain programs may need additional screening forms.
